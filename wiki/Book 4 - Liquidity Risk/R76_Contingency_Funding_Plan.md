# R76 — Contingency Funding Plan

**Exam Priority:** 🟡 Medium (1-2 questions)ning

## 1. Foundational Propositions
| ID | Proposition | Mapping | Intuition |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| `P.76.1` | **CFP Purpose:** A formal "Playbook" for **High-severity, Low-frequency** liquidity events. It uses the results of Liquidity Stress Tests (LST) and Early Warning Indicators (EWIs) as triggers for action. | `[OPS]` | It’s the "Emergency Manual" in the airplane cockpit. You don't read it during a normal flight; you pull it out when both engines fail. |
| `P.76.2` | **The LCT Oversight:** The **Liquidity Crisis Team (LCT)** is responsible for managing the crisis, and it is directly supervised by the **Management Committee** (not the Board, though the Board advises). | `[OPS]` | The LCT are the "Firefighters." The Management Committee is the "Fire Chief" giving immediate orders. The Board is the "Town Council" watching from a distance. |
| `P.76.3` | **Escalation Level 2 — The Analytical Pivot:** Level 2 is the stage where the crisis is clear, and the focus shifts to a **detailed analysis of the causes of deterioration** and how competitors are reacting. | `[OPS]` | Level 1 is "Wait and See." Level 2 is "Why is this happening and what is J.P. Morgan doing about it?" |
| `P.76.4` | **Escalation Level 3 — Survival Mode:** The focus is purely on **remaining a going concern**. Actions become drastic: selling less liquid assets and severely reducing new loan-granting activities. | `[OPS]` | This is "Lifeboat Mode." You stop taking on new passengers (no new loans) and you start throwing the cargo overboard (selling illiquid assets) to stay afloat. |
| `P.76.5` | **Contingent Actions vs. Business Grinders:** Actions like **Securitizing Assets** are positive contingent actions (inflow); actions like "Decreasing lending rates" are negative drivers (outflow) that worsen liquidity. | `[ECO/OPS]` | A contingent action must **Bring Cash In**. Lowering rates to win customers for loans *drains* cash, which is suicide in a liquidity crisis. |

## 2. Constraint Stress-Tests (Falsification)
- **Variable Flip: If a bank uses its CFP for "High-frequency" small liquidity gaps:** This is **Incorrect**. CFPs are reserved for **Extreme/Stress** states. Normal daily gaps are handled by the Treasury's standard "Business as Usual" (BAU) unit.
- **Variable Flip: If the Board of Directors has "Direct Oversight" of the LCT:** This is a governance mismatch. In the exam, direct oversight belongs to the **Management Committee**. The Board's role is higher-level approval and strategic alignment.
- **Variable Flip: If a bank increases "Capital Distributions" (Dividends) during Level 3:** This is a violation of crisis management logic. In a crisis, you **Cease** dividends to preserve cash. Increasing them would be a fast track to insolvency.

## 3. Dependency & Noise Map
| Signal (Exam Core) | Noise (Distractors) |
|:---|:---|
| **Level 2:** Detailed analysis of causes. | **Level 1:** Detailed analysis of causes (NO: that's just increased oversight). |
| **Management Committee:** Direct oversight of LCT. | **Risk Management:** Has oversight (NO: they are 2LoD, not part of the command committee). |
| **Securitization:** Increases liquidity (Contingent inflow). | **Asset Growth:** Reduces liquidity (Standard outflow). |

## 4. Directional Intuition
- **Crisis Severity $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Escalation Level $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Drastic Actions:** As you move from Level 1 to 3, the "Sacrifices" (selling assets at deep discounts) become larger.
- **Lending Rates $\uparrow$ (Spread) $\rightarrow$ New Asset Growth $\downarrow \rightarrow$ Cash Preservation $\uparrow$:** In a crisis, the bank stops trying to grow and focuses on holding onto every dollar.
- **Communication Quality $\uparrow \rightarrow$ External Confidence $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Run Pressure $\downarrow$:** A key part of the CFP is the **Communication Plan** to reassure stakeholders and prevent the panic from worsening.

## 5. Ambiguity Traps (Anti-Decoder)
- **LCT Oversight:** Who is the boss? **Management Committee.** (Don't pick Board).
- **Escalation Levels:** 
    - 1 = Watch. 
    - 2 = Analyze "Why" + Peers. 
    - 3 = Survive at all costs.
- **Contingent Inflows:** Look for **Securitization** or **Asset Sales**. Look out for "Decreasing lending rates" as a distractor (this is a cash drain).
- **CFP Timing:** Is it for normal times? **NO.** Stress states only.
- **Peers:** Does a bank care about peers in Level 2? **YES.** Their response tells you if the problem is just you (idiosyncratic) or everyone (systemic).
- **Testing:** Does a CFP just sit on a shelf? **NO.** It must be **Regularly Tested**.
