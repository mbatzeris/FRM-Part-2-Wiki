# R103 — Geopolitical Stability

**Exam Priority:** 🔴 High (3-4 questions) and Financial Stability

## 1. Foundational Propositions
| ID | Proposition | Mapping | Intuition |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| `P.103.1` | **Geopolitical Risk Transmission:** Shocks move through three channels: (1) **Physical** (infrastructure damage), (2) **Macroeconomic** (supply chains and commodity prices), and (3) **Financial** (capital flight and risk-off sentiment). | `[ECO/MKT]` | It’s a "Tripwire" effect. One border conflict (Physical) slows down the ships (Macro), which makes investors panic and pull their money out of the whole region (Financial). |
| `P.103.2` | **The Safe Haven Effect:** During geopolitical crises, capital flows away from "Source" economies and toward "Safe Havens" (Gold, USD, Swiss Franc). This leads to currency depreciation and increased borrowing costs for the affected countries. | `[MKT/ECO]` | When the "Neighborhood" gets dangerous, everyone puts their cash in their pockets and moves to a "Vault" in another city. |
| `P.103.3` | **EM Vulnerability:** Emerging Market (EM) economies with high debt-to-GDP, low fiscal buffers, and weak institutional quality are the most sensitive to geopolitical shocks. Their **Sovereign CDS Spreads** spike the hardest. | `[MKT/ECO]` | If you are already "Sick" (high debt), you can't handle a "Cold" (geopolitical stress) as well as a healthy person (low debt) can. |
| `P.103.4` | **Sectoral Winners & Losers:** While most firms suffer, **Defense** and **Energy** (exporters) often see stock price gains during geopolitical escalations as military spending increases and commodity prices rise. | `[MKT/ECO]` | War is bad for the "Tourist" business, but it's good for the "Tank" builder and the "Oil" driller. |
| `P.103.5` | **Fund Redemption Pressure:** Investment funds with exposure to conflict zones face "First-Mover" redemption pressures from investors. Because central banks do not provide direct liquidity to these funds, they may be forced to sell assets at a loss. | `[OPS/MKT]` | If an investor thinks a fund is going to crash, they try to get their money out *today* before the doors are locked. This forced selling makes the crash happen. |

## 2. Constraint Stress-Tests (Falsification)
- **Variable Flip: If a firm has no DIRECT exposure to a conflict zone:** It can still be **Decimated** through **Indirect Channels**. Disruptions to critical supply chains (e.g., Neon gas for chips) or broader market liquidity shortfalls can sink any firm.
- **Variable Flip: If a country is a major Commodity Importer (like Japan/Europe) during a resource shock:** It faces massive **Inflationary Pressure** and current account deficits, even if the conflict is far away.
- **Variable Flip: If a central bank intervenes to peg the currency during a geopolitical shock:** They might burn through all their **Foreign Exchange Reserves** very quickly, leading to a later, even more catastrophic currency collapse.

## 3. Dependency & Noise Map
| Signal (Exam Core) | Noise (Distractors) |
|:---|:---|
| **Sovereign CDS Spreads:** Increase significantly for EMs. | **Corporate Equity:** Always increases in value (NO: mostly decreases). |
| **GPR Index:** Newspaper-based measure of geopolitical risk. | **VIX Index:** Market-based measure of volatility (NO: VIX is broad, GPR is specific). |
| **Financial Fragmentation:** Capital flow segregation by political blocs. | **Financial Integration:** Increased cross-border flows (NO: geopolitics *breaks* integration). |

## 4. Directional Intuition
- **Institutional Quality $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Geopolitical Resilience $\uparrow$:** Strong laws and stable governments act as a "Bumper" that protects the economy from external shocks.
- **Energy Net Exports $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Geopolitical Gain (Relative) $\uparrow$:** High-oil prices transfer wealth from the "Drivers" (Importers) to the "Drillers" (Exporters).
- **Public Debt $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Sovereign Risk Spikes $\uparrow \uparrow$:** Markets lose faith quickly in over-leveraged governments when trouble starts.

## 5. Ambiguity Traps (Anti-Decoder)
- **Direct vs. Indirect:** Exam questions often try to trick you into saying a firm is "Safe" because it has no offices in the war zone. **Wrong.** Indirect supply chain ties are lethal.
- **Winners:** Don't assume everyone loses. **Defense** and **Commodity** sectors are counter-cyclical here.
- **The "Safety Haven" Goldmine:** Gold and USD are the classic "Risk-off" assets.
- **Geopolitical Risk Index (GPR):** It’s a "Text-based" index (newspaper analysis), not a "Market-based" one.
- **Liquidity support:** Central banks support **Banks**, but generally NOT **Investment Funds**. This is a major structural weakness for funds.
- **CDS Spreads:** A measure of "Default Insurance" cost. High spread = High fear.
- **Diversification:** Does "Revenue Diversification" save you? **Partially**, but if your main supply chain is cut, all your revenue streams might dry up together.
- **Macro-financial loops:** Geopolitical risk $\rightarrow$ Market Volatility $\rightarrow$ Lower Investment $\rightarrow$ Higher Debt service $\rightarrow$ Recession.
- **Fragmentation:** The world "splitting" into political blocs (e.g., US-led vs China-led networks).
