# R92 — The Rise and Risks of Private Credit

**Exam Priority:** 🟡 Medium (1-2 questions)

## 1. Foundational Propositions
| ID | Proposition | Mapping | Intuition |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| `P.92.1` | **Closed-End Dominance:** Over 80% of the private credit market consists of closed-end funds with a capital-call structure. This matches the illiquid nature of the loans and prevents "run" risk. | `[OPS/THE]` | You can't ask for your money back tomorrow. Your money is "locked in" the vault because the lender can't just sell the small company's loan instantly. |
| `P.92.2` | **The Performance Sweet Spot:** Private credit has historically outperformed the S&P 500 and Venture Capital (post-2020) while maintaining lower volatility than High-Yield bonds. | `[MKT/ECO]` | It’s the "Middle Child." It's more stable than stocks but pays better than traditional corporate bonds, provided you can handle not seeing your money for 7 years. |
| `P.92.3` | **Credit Loss Mitigation:** Losses in private credit are lower than High-Yield bonds because (1) loans are almost always **Collateralized** and (2) borrowers are often **PE-Sponsored** (the PE fund can inject cash to save the company). | `[THE/OPS]` | If the company trips, it has a "Rich Uncle" (PE Fund) to catch it, and if it fails completely, you own the building and equipment (Collateral). |
| `P.92.4` | **Valuation "Smoothing" Risk:** Because it is a private market with no daily ticker, managers might "defer" write-downs. This masks volatility but creates a "Domino Risk" if all losses are realized at once during a crisis. | `[MKT/OPS]` | It's like checking your weight only once a month. You look "steady" on the chart, but you might have gained 10 pounds in the second week without knowing it. |
| `P.92.5` | **Interconnectedness Risk:** The Private Credit and Private Equity industries are inextricably linked. A failure in one (e.g., a massive capital call) can quickly spill over into the other, creating a systemic shock. | `[ECO/MKT]` | They are two climbers on the same rope. If the PE climber falls, he might yank the Credit climber off the cliff with him. |

## 2. Constraint Stress-Tests (Falsification)
- **Variable Flip: If Private Credit funds allowed "Daily Redemptions":** Then they would face massive **Liquidity Mismatches** and "Run Risk," similar to open-end mutual funds during a panic.
- **Variable Flip: If interest rates RISE sharply:** Private credit borrowers (who usually have **Floating Rate** debt) face higher debt-service costs. This could lead to a spike in defaults among the highly-leveraged companies that use this market.
- **Variable Flip: If a borrower is NOT PE-sponsored:** Their probability of default is higher. The "PE Support" is a major invisible hedge in the private credit market.

## 3. Dependency & Noise Map
| Signal (Exam Core) | Noise (Distractors) |
|:---|:---|
| **Closed-end Fund:** Standard structure (80%). | **BDCs / CLOs:** Standard structure (NO: they are minorities, ~14% and ~5%). |

## 4. Directional Intuition
- **Interest Rates $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Borrower Default Risk $\uparrow$:** Highly leveraged firms struggle to pay the higher "spread" on their floating-rate loans.
- **PE Performance $\downarrow \rightarrow$ Private Credit Recovery $\downarrow$:** If the "sponsor" is in trouble, they can't afford to save the borrower.
- **Manager Competition $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Underwriting Covenants $\downarrow$:** As too much money chases too few deals ("Dry Powder"), lenders get sloppy (Covenant-lite).

## 5. Ambiguity Traps (Anti-Decoder)
- **The "80%" Rule:** Most private credit is **Closed-end**. Don't be fooled by the high-profile nature of BDCs (Business Development Companies); they are a small slice of the pie.
- **Loss Comparison:** Private credit losses $\approx$ Leveraged Loans < High-Yield bonds.
- **Floating Rate:** Does private credit have "Rate Risk"? **YES**, but for the *borrower* (default risk), not necessarily the *lender's* market value (since coupons reset).
- **Transparency:** Is it better than banks? **NO.** Much worse. It’s the "Hidden" part of the credit system.
- **Valuation:** Is the low volatility real? **Partially.** But much of it is **Mark-to-Model smoothing**.
- **Conduct Risk:** The risk that retail investors don't understand that their money is effectively "locked up" for years.
- **Capital Calls:** If a fund asks for more money (capital call) during a crisis when the investor is also broke, the whole system breaks down.
- **Sponsorship:** PE firms often own the equity of the firms that Private Credit lenders are lending to.
