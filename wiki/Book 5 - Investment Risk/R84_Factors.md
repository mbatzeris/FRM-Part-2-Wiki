# R84 — Factors

**Exam Priority:** 🟡 Medium (1-2 questions)

## 1. Foundational Propositions
| ID | Proposition | Mapping | Intuition |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| `P.84.1` | **The Value Premium:** Value stocks (High Book-to-Market) historically outperform Growth stocks (Low B/M). Rationalists see this as a reward for distress risk; Behavioralists see it as a correction for over-optimism about "Growth" firms. | `[THE/ECO]` | Value stocks are "ugly" companies that pay you to own them because they might fail. Growth stocks are "glamour" companies that people overpay for because they love the story. |
| `P.84.2` | **The Macro Trio:** Economic Growth, Inflation, and Volatility are the three primary macroeconomic factors. Critically, it is **unanticipated changes** in these factors that move prices, not their absolute levels. | `[MKT/ECO]` | If everyone expects 5% inflation, a 5% reading won't move the needle. A surprise jump to 8% is what causes the market to reprice. |
| `P.84.3` | **Fama-French Factors (SMB/HML):** Beyond the Market (CAPM), returns are driven by **Size** (Small Minus Big) and **Value** (High Minus Low). | `[MTH/THE]` | Small firms and cheap firms are inherently riskier than "Microsofts" and "Glamour" stocks, so they must offer higher expected returns to attract capital. |
| `P.84.4` | **Momentum — The Destabilizer:** Momentum is a "Positive Feedback" strategy (buy winners, sell losers). It is inherently destabilizing because it pushes prices further away from fundamental values. | `[THE]` | Momentum is like a "Stampede." Everyone runs where everyone else is running. Value is the "Contrarian" (Stabilizer) who steps in when the price gets too far from reality. |
| `P.84.5` | **Volatility Protection:** Investors can mitigate volatility risk by shifting to bonds or buying OTM puts. Selling volatility (writing options) earns a "Volatility Premium" but exposes the investor to "Tail Risk" during crashes. | `[OPS/MKT]` | You can either pay for "Insurance" (buying puts) or act as the "Insurance Company" (selling puts) and collect the premiums—until the "Big One" hits. |

## 2. Constraint Stress-Tests (Falsification)
- **Variable Flip: If an investor expects high inflation:** They should shift away from both Stocks and Bonds, as both typically perform poorly in high-inflation regimes.
- **Variable Flip: If a firm has a Low Book-to-Market ratio:** It is a **Growth** stock. It is expected to have lower returns than a Value stock (HML factor logic).
- **Variable Flip: If a central bank suddenly changes its policy path:** This is a **Momentum Interrupter**. Momentum crashes are often triggered by policy shifts that break the existing price trend.

## 3. Dependency & Noise Map
| Signal (Exam Core) | Noise (Distractors) |
|:---|:---|
| **Value (HML):** Long High B/M, Short Low B/M. | **Value:** Long Low B/E (NO: it's B/M, not PE). |
| **Momentum (WML):** Positive feedback / Destabilizing. | **Value:** Positive feedback (NO: Value is negative-feedback / Stabilizing). |
| **MACRO Impact:** Driven by *unanticipated* shocks. | **MACRO Impact:** Driven by absolute *levels* of inflation (NO: shocks only). |

## 4. Directional Intuition
- **Economic Growth $\uparrow$ (Shock) $\rightarrow$ Risky Asset Prices $\uparrow$:** Surprise growth is a "tailwind" for risky assets.
- **Volatility (VIX) $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Stock Returns $\downarrow \downarrow$:** Increasing fear causes investors to dump equities for safety.
- **SMB Factor $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Small Caps Outperforming:** Small firms are winning the "Size" battle.

## 5. Ambiguity Traps (Anti-Decoder)
- **The "B/M" Ratio:** Value = **High** Book-to-Market (Price is low relative to accounting value). Growth = **Low** B/M.
- **SMB Alignment:** SMB = **Small Minus Big**. If SMB is positive, small stocks won.
- **HML Alignment:** HML = **High Minus Low** (Value). If HML is positive, cheap stocks won.
- **Momentum Horizon:** Usually looking at the last **6 to 12 months**. It’s not a "daily" scalp; it’s a trend-following duration.
- **Stabilization:** Which one stabilizes the market? **VALUE**. (It buys when low, sells when high).
- **High-Yield Bonds in Recession:** Note that they are surprisingly indifferent (similar returns in both). Government/IG bonds are the ones that truly outperfom in recessions.
- **Volatility Premiums:** Who earns them? The **Option SELLER**.
- **WML discovers:** Jegadeesh and Titman (distinguish from Fama-French SMB/HML).
