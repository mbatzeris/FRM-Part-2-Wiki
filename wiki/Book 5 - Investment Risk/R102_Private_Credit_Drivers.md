# R102 — The Global Drivers of Private Credit

**Exam Priority:** 🔴 High (3-4 questions) of Private Credit

## 1. Foundational Propositions
| ID | Proposition | Mapping | Intuition |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| `P.102.1` | **The BIS Growth Drivers:** The expansion of private credit is driven by four factors: (1) Low Banking Efficiency, (2) Accommodative Monetary Policy, (3) Regulatory Stringency on Banks, and (4) Rising Corporate Leverage. | `[ECO/REG]` | Private credit is the "Shadow" that grows when the "Sun" (Banking efficiency) goes down. When banks get too regulated or slow, PCFs step in to fill the gap. |
| `P.102.2` | **FI Index Dominance:** Banking sector efficiency (measured by the IMF's Financial Institutions index) has the **Largest impact** on private credit growth. A decline in bank efficiency is the #1 predictor of PC expansion. | `[MKT/ECO]` | If the local bank is too lazy or too small to lend, that’s when the "Private Credit" fund thrives. It’s a direct competitor to bank inefficiency. |
| `P.102.3` | **Narrowing Funding Advantage:** The gap between bank funding costs and BDC funding costs has closed. This is primarily due to the **rising Cost of Equity for banks** (due to regulation) and PCFs/BDCs shifting their mix toward **Cheaper Debt financing** (higher leverage). | `[MTH/ECO]` | Banks used to have "Free Money" (Deposits) that made them unbeatable. Now, they have "Expensive Rules" that make their equity costly, while BDCs have figured out how to borrow cheaply from the bond market. |
| `P.102.4` | **BDC Structure (The Retail Slice):** Business Development Companies represent 20% of the US private credit market. Unlike standard total-lockup funds, BDCs are often publicly listed and offer redemptions to retail investors. | `[OPS/REG]` | They are the "Public Face" of the private credit world. They let your neighbor invest in small business loans through their brokerage account. |
| `P.102.5` | **Tailored Covenants:** The primary attraction of private credit for borrowers isn't just "speed," but the ability to negotiate specific, bespoke covenants that traditional banks (with their "one-size-fits-all" rules) cannot offer. | `[THE/OPS]` | It’s "Custom Tailored" debt. A bank gives you a suit off the rack; a PCF builds the suit to fit your specific (and often weird) business shape. |

## 2. Constraint Stress-Tests (Falsification)
- **Variable Flip: If the policy rate RISES sharply:** BDCs might lose their "Cheap debt" advantage. Their overall cost of capital would rise, potentially making them less competitive against traditional banks again.
- **Variable Flip: If bank "Cost of Equity" drops (due to deregulation):** Banks could reclaim their traditional market share, as their overall "Pricing Power" would increase relative to private credit funds.
- **Variable Flip: If a PCF has a highly concentrated sector portfolio (e.g., all 100% in Tech):** It might have great monitoring, but it is **highly vulnerable** to a sector-specific downturn. The diversification benefit of the asset class is lost at the individual fund level.

## 3. Dependency & Noise Map
| Signal (Exam Core) | Noise (Distractors) |
|:---|:---|
| **Primary Driver:** Banking Efficiency (IMF FI Index). | **Primary Driver:** Corporate Leverage (NO: it’s the smallest driver, <1%). |
| **Funding Gap Shift:** Higher bank Cost of Equity. | **Funding Gap Shift:** Higher bank Cost of Debt (NO: difference in debt cost remained small). |
| **PC Preference:** Flexible / Bespoke covenants. | **PC Preference:** Lower interest rates (NO: individual loan rates are often higher). |

## 4. Directional Intuition
- **Bank Efficiency $\downarrow \rightarrow$ Private Credit AUM $\uparrow \uparrow$:** (33% move for 1-std dev). This is the "Nuclear Engine" of PC growth.
- **Policy Rates $\uparrow \rightarrow$ PC Growth $\downarrow$:** Lower rates encourage "reach for yield" in private credit; higher rates pull capital back to "safer" liquid alternatives.
- **Bank Regulation $\uparrow \rightarrow$ Private Credit Opportunity $\uparrow$:** The more "Capital" a bank must hold for a loan, the more it has to charge, and the easier it is for a PCF to beat them on price or flexibility.

## 5. Ambiguity Traps (Anti-Decoder)
- **The "Leverage" Twist:** PCF growth is actually **correlated** with bank leverage (when banks are levered up, they often push risk to the shadows).
- **BDC Redemption:** Don't assume all private credit is "locked up." BDCs represent the **liquid** exception.
- **Cost of Debt (CoD):** Did BDC debt get cheaper than bank debt? **NO.** It stayed more expensive, but the *overall* mix improved for BDCs relative to the *skyrocketing* equity costs of banks.
- **FI Index:** IMF's measure of "Depth, Access, and Efficiency."
- **Institutional vs. Retail:** PCFs are for big guys; BDCs are for everyone.
- **Floating Rate:** Like Reading 92, remember that PC is mostly floating. This means the *Lender* is protected from rate hikes, but the *Borrower* might go bankrupt.
- **Execution Speed:** PCFs can fund in weeks; banks often take months.
- **Information Asymmetry:** PCFs thrive on "opaque" borrowers that banks don't have the stomach or the "data" to handle.
- **Concentration:** Even though the *market* is big, individual *funds* are often very concentrated in specific industries (to maintain the "Expertise" advantage).
